Rule for Labelling News Articles

  • Consider the price of a share of stock S(di) on date di
  • Daily return r(di) for date di is equal to S(di+1)/S(di)
  • Based on a series of r(di) values construct a lognormal model of stock price returns
  • Identify the 10th percentile and the 90th percentile of r(di)
  • Consider a news article N(di) published on date di
  • if r(di) > 90th percentile then label as "Buy"
  • if r(di) < 10th percentile then label as "Sell"
  • if neither "Hold"




Distribution of Aggregate Buy/Sell Counts (575 stocks)
  • Since stock prices of the 575 stocks under consideration may not be independent random variables, one cannot use a trinomial distribution of independent trials for calculating percentiles of counts
  • To address the correlation among stock prices, a multi-variate normal distribution was utilized to study the interaction between 'buy' and 'sell' counts
  • Using the stock prices in the data set a multi-variate normal distiribution was modelled, from which 10,000 simulated values of buy-sell pairs were generated.
  • This simulated data set was used to observe the conditional distribution of buy/sell counts while fixing the value of sell/buy respectively.
  • The 90th percentile of sell/buy counts so obtained were used to assign "Buy", "Sell" or "Hold" for a given date.
